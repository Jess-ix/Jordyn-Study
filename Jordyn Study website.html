Introduction to Pathophysiology

Topic Description
Operational Definitions
  • Anatomy: The study of the structure of the body
  • Physiology: The study of the function of the body
  • Pathology: The study of diseases/injuries
  • Pathophysiology: The study of deviations from the physiological norm that result in a diseased/injured state
4 Central Concepts
  • Etiology: The cause of a disease
  • Pathogenesis: The mechanism/sequence of events that result in a diseased/injurious state
  • Morphology: Appearance of the disease
  • Functional Consequences: Clinical manifestation (signs and symptoms)

Cellular Injuries

Topic Description
8 Common Functions of the Cells
  • Movement
  • Conductivity
  • Metabolic absorption
  • Secretion
  • Excretion
  • Respiration
  • Reproduction
  • Communication
Cell Injury Mechanisms
  • Hypoxic Injury: Reduced oxygen supply to tissues
  • Free Radical Injury: Damage from reactive oxygen species
  • Chemical Injury: Toxins disrupting cellular functions
  • Nutritional Imbalance: Deficiency or excess of nutrients
  • Physical Agents: Mechanical trauma, temperature extremes
  • Infectious Agents: Bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites
Cellular Adaptations
  • Atrophy: Decrease in cell size and function
  • Hypertrophy: Increase in cell size
  • Hyperplasia: Increase in cell number
  • Metaplasia: Replacement of one cell type with another
  • Dysplasia: Abnormal changes in cell size, shape, and organization

Inflammation and Repair

Topic Description
Acute Inflammation
  • Cardinal Signs: Redness, heat, swelling, pain, loss of function
  • Vascular Changes: Vasodilation, increased permeability
  • Cellular Events: Neutrophil recruitment, phagocytosis
  • Chemical Mediators: Histamine, prostaglandins, cytokines
Chronic Inflammation
  • Characteristics: Prolonged duration, mononuclear cell infiltration
  • Cellular Components: Macrophages, lymphocytes, plasma cells
  • Tissue Effects: Fibrosis, granuloma formation
  • Systemic Effects: Fatigue, weight loss, fever
Inflammatory Mediators
  • Cell-derived: Histamine, cytokines, lysosomal enzymes
  • Plasma-derived: Complement, kinins, clotting factors
  • Lipid Mediators: Prostaglandins, leukotrienes

Healing and Regeneration

Topic Description
Cell Regeneration
  • Labile Cells: Continuously divide (e.g., epithelial cells)
  • Stable Cells: Normally quiescent but can divide (e.g., liver cells)
  • Permanent Cells: Cannot divide (e.g., neurons, cardiac muscle)
Wound Healing
  • Primary Intention: Clean, well-approximated wound edges
  • Secondary Intention: Larger wounds with tissue loss
  • Phases: Hemostasis, inflammation, proliferation, remodeling
  • Factors Affecting Healing: Nutrition, age, tissue perfusion, infection
Tissue Repair
  • Regeneration: Replacement with identical tissue
  • Fibrosis: Replacement with connective tissue/scar
  • Growth Factors: PDGF, EGF, FGF, TGF-Î²

Neoplasia and Cancer

Topic Description
Characteristics of Neoplasia
  • Definition: New growth; abnormal mass of tissue with excessive proliferation
  • Benign: Non-invasive, well-differentiated, slow-growing
  • Malignant: Invasive, poorly differentiated, metastatic potential
Carcinogenesis
  • Initiation: DNA damage by carcinogens
  • Promotion: Clonal expansion of initiated cells
  • Progression: Acquisition of malignant characteristics
  • Carcinogens: Chemical, radiation, viral, genetic
Tumor Classification
  • Carcinoma: Epithelial origin
  • Sarcoma: Mesenchymal origin
  • Leukemia/Lymphoma: Hematopoietic origin
  • Grading: Degree of differentiation (I-IV)
  • Staging: Extent of spread (TNM system)

Sample Answer:

Acute inflammation is a rapid, short-term response characterized by neutrophil infiltration, vascular changes, and the cardinal signs (redness, heat, swelling, pain, loss of function). Sample Answer:

Healing by primary intention occurs in clean wounds with well-approximated edges. It involves minimal granulation tissue formation and proceeds through four phases: hemostasis (blood clotting), inflammation, proliferation (formation of granulation tissue), and remodeling (scar maturation).
